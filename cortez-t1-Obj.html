Objective

  • Investigate usage patterns: To explore the specific ways advanced English language students utilize smartphones and tablet computers for their language learning processes.
  • Examine learner autonomy: To understand the connection between mobile technologies and the ability of learners to engage in independent practice and "critical adaptive learning."
  • Analyze learning environments: To comprehend how language learners organize their own learning experiences and environments through the use of mobile devices.
  • Identify technological affordances: To determine how the unique features of mobile devices—such as individualized learning and app variety—serve as useful additions to both formal and informal education.
  • Bridge the gap between tools and practice: To align future inquiry into technology-mediated autonomy with the specific tools and activities that are significant to modern language learners.
  • Inform pedagogical preparation: To provide insights that help teachers equip students with the knowledge and skills necessary to use mobile devices effectively for language study.
  • Contribute to the literature: To offer a structured overview of relevant research and present new data regarding the role of smartphones and tablets in contemporary language acquisition.
