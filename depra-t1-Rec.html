The study recommends regular monitoring of the Marikina River to track microbial contamination and antibiotic resistance trends.

Improved wastewater treatment and sanitation practices should be enforced to reduce fecal pollution and the spread of resistant bacteria.

Proper antibiotic stewardship in human health, agriculture, and veterinary sectors is essential to limit environmental contamination.

Public awareness campaigns should be strengthened to inform communities about the risks of exposure to contaminated river water.

Further research, including molecular analysis of resistance genes, is encouraged to better understand the sources and transmission of antibiotic resistance.
