Methodology

1. Field Sampling of Bivalves and Water

Collection of multiple commercial bivalve species (e.g., mussels and clams) from different coastal sites with contrasting levels of human impact (anthropized vs. less impacted areas). 

Sampling of surface water from aquaculture zones and nearby ocean waters where these bivalves live.  2. Laboratory Preparation

Dissection of bivalves to isolate soft tissues where microplastics accumulate (e.g., gills, digestive tract). This is standard in microplastic bioaccumulation studies. 

Pre-treatment of tissues and water samples to remove organic matter (e.g., through digestion with enzymes or chemicals) before microplastic extraction.

3. Microplastic Extraction

Density separation using saline or heavy solutions (e.g., NaCl or ZnCl₂) to float microplastics away from biological material. This is a common method in microplastic research to separate plastics from tissue/water matrices. 

4. Visual Isolation and Identification

Microscopic examination under a stereo microscope to count and size microplastic particles. 

Classification of particles by shape (e.g., fibers, fragments) and color.

5. Polymer Characterization

Use of spectroscopic analysis such as ATR-FTIR (Attenuated Total Reflection Fourier-Transform Infrared Spectroscopy) to identify types of plastic polymers present (e.g., polyamide, polyester, polyethylene). 

6. Comparative Analysis

Quantification of microplastic abundance in bivalves and water.

Comparison between sites (anthropized vs. less impacted) and among species to investigate differences in microplastic load and characteristics.
