Results

Main Findings

Microplastics were consistently detected in all sampled bivalve species and in surface waters from bivalve aquaculture areas, even though the overall concentrations were relatively low.

Higher microplastic levels occurred in more anthropogenically impacted sites, indicating that human activity increases microplastic contamination in both organisms and surrounding waters.

Fibers were the predominant type of microplastic found in both bivalves and water samples.

The most common polymer types identified were polyamide, polyester, and polyethylene, which are typical materials from synthetic textiles and packaging.

Interspecific differences in microplastic content were observed among the bivalve species studied—likely linked to differences in feeding behaviour or habitat—suggesting that not all species accumulate microplastics the same way.
