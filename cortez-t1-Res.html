Results


The results of the study indicate that smartphones are the primary tool for advanced English learners, who value them for their convenience, speed, and portability in managing vast amounts of study material. While most participants reported an increase in mobile usage over an average of 3.8 years, a significant 45% still lack confidence in their technical proficiency for language learning. The data shows a strong preference for informal, student-led learning during leisure time, with vocabulary development and dictionary use being the dominant activities. Despite a lack of teacher-led integration in formal classes, 75% of students felt that mobile devices significantly improved their study performance, citing increased efficiency and engagement—particularly for kinesthetic learners—as the primary benefits of these digital tools.
