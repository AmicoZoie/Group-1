Objective

  • Analyze Prescribing Trends: To describe the shifts in direct-acting oral anticoagulant (DOAC) prescribing patterns following the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic using the OpenSAFELY-TPP platform.
  • Evaluate Clinical Record Completeness: To determine the proportion of patients prescribed a DOAC who have essential clinical parameters recorded in their electronic health records, specifically:
    • Body weight
    • Serum creatinine
    • Estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR)
    • Creatinine clearance (CrCl)
  • Assess Guideline Adherence: To identify if these clinical parameters are being recorded in accordance with national recommendations for calculating accurate DOAC doses.
  • Identify Patient Safety Risks: To investigate whether the use of eGFR (which can overestimate renal function) instead of the recommended Cockcroftâ€“Gault method (CrCl) is contributing to inappropriate dosing and increased bleeding risks.
  • Examine Specific Patient Groups: To look at recording rates and dosing accuracy specifically in high-risk groups, such as individuals with atrial fibrillation (AF) who require long-term anticoagulation.
