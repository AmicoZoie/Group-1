Objectives

  • To provide an overview of Staphylococcus aureus biofilm formation and its significance in clinical infections
  • To describe the stages involved in the biofilm formation process of S. aureus
  • To explain the structural and functional components of the biofilm extracellular matrix
  • To review the molecular mechanisms involved in S. aureus biofilm formation
  • To discuss the regulatory systems controlling biofilm development, maturation, and dispersion
  • To highlight the role of biofilm formation in antibiotic resistance and persistent infections
  • To identify potential targets for developing effective antibiofilm and therapeutic strategies
