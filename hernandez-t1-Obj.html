Objectives

  • To review recent advances in paper-based electroactive materials used for flexible and sustainable energy storage applications, particularly in wearable and portable electronics.
  • To classify and analyze paper-based electrode architectures, specifically paper-supported electrodes and freestanding paper-like structures, highlighting their structural and functional differences.
  • To examine fabrication strategies and material design approaches for paper-based electrodes, including direct loading, in situ growth, and composite engineering techniques.
  • To evaluate the electrochemical performance of paper-based energy storage devices, with particular emphasis on paper-based supercapacitors, considering metrics such as energy density, power density, flexibility, and cycling stability.
  • To assess the role of cellulose and carbon-based materials (e.g., graphene, CNTs, carbon fibers, and cellulose nanofibers) in enhancing mechanical flexibility, conductivity, and charge storage capability.
