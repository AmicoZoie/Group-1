Objective

Primary Objective

To investigate microplastic pollution by measuring the presence, types, and characteristics of microplastics in both commercially important bivalve species and in surface waters from bivalve aquaculture zones in the southern Pacific.

Secondary/Specific Aims

To compare microplastic concentrations in bivalves and aquaculture waters from areas with different levels of human (anthropogenic) impact.

To characterize the types of microplastics (e.g., polymer types and physical forms such as fibers) present in both biological and environmental samples.

To provide insights useful for monitoring and mitigation strategies, highlighting the need to monitor microplastics in aquaculture and possibly inform management practices.
