Results

• First study of its kind at coastal hawker stalls

This research measured how microplastics (tiny plastic particles <5 mm) settle out of the air (dry deposition) in different environments around hawker stalls — suburban, urban, and industrial coastal areas.

• Abundance & distribution of airborne microplastics

Microplastic deposition rates varied widely across locations, ranging from about 0.48 to 17.44 particles per square meter per day.

This indicates people visiting or working at these coastal hawker stalls are likely exposed to microplastics in the air around them.

• Types of microplastics identified

The majority of airborne microplastics were fibers, far more common than fragments.

Among polymers identified:

PET (polyester) was dominant (~81 %)

Nylon (polyamide) was also present (~5 %)

•Physical characteristics

Transparent microplastic fibers were the most commonly found, followed by black, purple, and brown fibers.
